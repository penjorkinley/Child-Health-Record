Key Information For Infant And Child Safety
  • General Advice For All Ages

    • Get all of your baby's immunization at their scheduled times
    • Bring your baby to be weighed and measured monthly for the first year of life
    • From 1 year of age until your child enters school, bring your child every three months to be weighed and measured, and for necessary vitamin supplementation and immunization
    • If your child does not appear to be developing like other children his/her age, have them seen at the local health center
    • Use a car seat for your infant or child when possible
    • Infants and children should be in the back seat of a vehicle, and never be allowed to stick their head or arms outside a moving vehicle
    • Do not leave your infant or child unattended
    • Do not smoke near your infant or child


  • Birth To 5 Months

    • Do not give your baby water, or food before 6 months of age as it increases the risk of diarrhea or choking
    • Do not cover your baby's face with a blanket to prevent suffocation
    • Do not leave your baby on his/her belly unattended


  • 6 months to 2 years

    • Do not give a child hard foods or candy, small toys with detachable pieces, or objects smaller than their fist as they are choking risks
    • When child starts rolling, crawling or walking, protect them from falling out of bed, down stairs, out of windows or off of balconies
    • Keep all medicines, cleaning supplies, pesticides and flammable liquids out of reach of child
    • Cove buckets of water to prevent drowning and keep sharp objects away from child
    • Baby walkers do not prepare/help baby to walk on his/her own and does not strengthen muscles


  • 2-5 years

    • Do not let child play with matches or around fires or hot liquids
    • Do not allow child to play unsupervised near rivers or roads
    • Do not allow child to play with sharp objects
    • Do not leave the child alone
